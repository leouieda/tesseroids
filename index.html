Welcome to Tesseroids

Tesseroids is a suite of open source programs for the forward modeling of gravitational fields in spherical coordinates. It can model the gravitational potential, acceleration, and gradient tensor. All programs run on the command-line and can be easily plugged into shell scripts and workflows.

So, what is a tesseroid?

The geometric element used in the modeling processes is a spherical prism, also called a tesseroid. Tesseroids are useful to model large geologic structures where the Earth's curvature must be taken into consideration. However, the Tesseroids package also contains programs for modeling using right rectangular prisms, both in Cartesian and spherical coordinates.

Authors and Contributors

Tesseroids is developed by myself (Leonardo Uieda) in cooperation with prof. Carla Braitenberg.

It was first developed as my bachelor's thesis at the Universidade de Sao Paulo, Brazil. It has now made it's way into my PhD thesis at Observatorio Nacional, Brazil.

I also develop other open-source scientific software. My main project these days is Fatiando a Terra, a package of various modeling and inversion methods for geophysics. Fatiando includes most of the functionality of Tesseroids and adds 3D plotting capabilities, courtesy of Mayavi.
